Welcome to Decision Manager and Mozu

CyberSource's Decision Manager provides a fraud protection platform that features the World's Largest Fraud Detection Radar and a flexible rules engine that lets you customize rules to suit your business. With Decision Manager, you can screen your orders for risk in an effort to prevent fraud.

The Mozu CyberSource Decision Manager Application seamlessly integrates your Decision Manager account with your site so that any order you receive on your site is automatically screened by Decision Manager. With the Mozu CyberSource Decision Manager Application, every order receives a fraud score and a validation result. Based on these metrics, orders are accepted, rejected, or flagged for further review.

Install the Mozu CyberSource Decision Manager Application on Your Tenant

After purchasing the Mozu CyberSource Decision Manager Application, call your sales or professional services representative and request installation on your tenant. Mozu staff will install the app to your tenant, making it available for further configuration and usage in your storefront.

This application includes the following functionality:

  • Validate shopper purchases against your Decision Manager rules to determine a fraud score and validation result.
  • Update the order status as pass, fail, or rejected (pending further review) per the calculated score and your Decision Manager rules.

When a shopper places an order:

  • Mozu sends information about the order to Decision Manager, including the shopper's browser type, IP address, device type, etc.
  • Decision Manager calculates a fraud score and determines a validation result taking into account the rules you set during configuration.
  • The result from Decision Manager feeds back into Mozu to mark an order as accepted, failed, or rejected. If rejected, the order data did not clearly calculate as accepted or failed. You can review and actively accept or reject these orders through Mozu.
  • Mozu notifies you when purchases have returned as failed or rejected. These orders require your review in the Orders module to accept the payment or cancel the order due to potential fraud.

Configuration Requirements

To configure the Mozu PowerReviews Enterprise Application, you must have the following:

  • The Mozu PowerReviews Enterprise Application installed on your tenant.
  • An active PowerReviews account.
  • Access to modify and update your storefront theme.

Note: If you want to integrate reviews from a Volusion site with your PowerReviews account, contact our support team. If you want to integrate reviews from a third-party service, contact PowerReviews or an implementation engineer.

Configure the Application

To integrate your PowerReview services with Mozu, enter your PowerReviews account information in your Mozu settings.

Configure PowerReviews Account Settings

  1. Log in to your PowerReviews account.
  2. Click Configuration»Configure Reviews to display the general configuration settings for your PowerReviews account.
  3. Note the Merchant Group ID, Merchant ID, Site ID, FTP Site, FTP username, FTP password, and ZIP File Name. You need to enter these settings in Mozu.
  4. Log in to Mozu Admin.
  5. On the Mozu Admin top bar, select Settings»Applications.
  6. Click Mozu PowerReviews Enterprise Application.
  7. Click the Configuration link to open configuration settings.
  8. Open the Settings tab.
  9. Click Add Merchant Group to add your PowerReview account information to Mozu.
    1. Enter the Merchant Group ID.
    2. Enter the FTP Username.
    3. Enter the FTP Password.
    4. In the PowerReview Import Frequency drop-down, choose a frequency for importing content from PowerReviews to your Mozu site.
    5. Click Show Advanced Settings.
    6. Enter the FTP Location using the FTP site from your PowerReviews account.
    7. Enter the PowerReview Import Filename using the ZIP file name from your PowerReviews account.
    8. Create a PowerReview Import Ready Filename of your choice. What does this do? Name?
  10. Click Add Mozu Site to configure PowerReviews for your storefront.
    1. Select your storefront from the Mozu Site drop-down.
    2. Choose to enable exports to PowerReviews of your product and order ratings using Enable Product Export and Enable Order Export. What's the difference between product export and order export?
    3. Enter the Merchant ID.
    4. Enter the Merchant Site ID.
    5. Enter the Locale. Need more guidance on this.
    6. Choose how much data to export using the Number of days to include in order export.
  11. Click Save.

Enable the App

After configuration, enable the Mozu PowerReviews Enterprise Application in Mozu and apply the PowerReviews theme to your storefront using Site Builder.

  1. On the Mozu Admin top bar, select Settings»Applications.
  2. Click Mozu PowerReviews Enterprise Application.
  3. Click Enable App.
  4. In Mozu Admin, click Site Builder.
  5. Hover over your site and select Theme.
  6. Hover over the PowerReviews theme and click Apply.
  7. Click Site Builder to return to the Site Builder landing page.
  8. Hover over your site and select Edit.
  9. Add a Write a Review page to your site. (seems like just a blank page)
  10. Add PowerReviews widgets to your Product page (see the Add Widgets to Your Storefront section of this guide for details).

Note: PowerReviews widgets may not display until after the first import of PowerReviews content.

Import and Export Data

After configuring your settings and enabling the app, perform a manual export and import of data in order to populate PowerReviews content on your storefront for the first time. After the initial export and import, you can rely on automatic exports and imports between PowerReviews and Mozu for further updates.

Export Data

You can export reviews data about your products and orders from Mozu to PowerReviews using the Catalog or Order pages in Mozu Admin.

  1. In Mozu Admin, click Catalog or Orders.
  2. Click Extensions»PowerReviews»Export.
  3. Click Export Now.

After you export data for the first time, Mozu automatically exports data based on the settings you set during configuration.

Import Data

You can import reviews data for your products using the Mozu PowerReviews Enterprise Application configuration settings.

  1. On the Mozu Admin top bar, select Settings»Applications.
  2. Click Mozu PowerReviews Enterprise Application.
  3. Click the Configuration link to open configuration settings.
  4. On the Import tab, click Import Now.

After you import data for the first time, Mozu automatically imports data based on the settings you set during configuration.

The Import tab in the application configuration settings displays a log of imports. By default, Mozu imports data files at 8:00 AM CST daily from PowerReviews. You can view imported ratings per product using the Catalog page in Mozu Admin (select Catalog»Inventory and search for a product). A Rating field displays in the Properties section, showing a calculated average of all PowerReview shopper ratings entered through your storefront, or syndicated from external sites if your PowerReview account provides syndication services. The PowerReviews storefront theme uses the Rating property as one option to facet and filter products on your storefront.

Add Widgets to Your Storefront

Shoppers interact with PowerReviews content in your storefront through widgets you add to pages. The following PowerReviews widgets are available in Site Builder for use on the Product page:

  • Review Snippet—Allows customers to enter reviews.
  • Q&A Snippet—Allows customers to ask questions.
  • Review Display—Displays reviews.
  • Q&A Display—Displays questions and answers.

To add a widget, you may need help with customized code for adding dropzones or JavaScript code. Your implementation partners and Mozu staff can help with this content.

  1. In Mozu Admin, select Site Builder.
  2. Hover over your theme and select Edit.
  3. In the list of pages, expand the Templates section and select Product.
  4. Enable View dropzones.
  5. Click Widgets and drag PowerReviews Product Reviews into a dropzone.
  6. A configuration window displays. Choose a widget type and specify tab details. You can stack multiple widget types on the same page.
  7. Click Save.

Congratulations! You can now preview PowerReviews functionality on your storefront.
