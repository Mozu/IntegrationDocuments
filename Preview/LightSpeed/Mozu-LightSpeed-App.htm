Welcome to LightSpeed and Mozu

LightSpeed Retail provides services to manage your inventory, point of sale, and reporting through your eCommerce storefront. Customers can browse and purchase products from any storefront on any device using multiple forms of payment (cash, credit, debit, in-store credit, gift cards, and more).

Mozu offers an app to leverage LightSpeed Retail services to manage your orders, sync your inventory, and extend your management systems. Through the app, you can map Mozu locations with LightSpeed shops, set import frequencies for Mozu, and manage data imports and exports. Exported data supports Products/Categories, Customers, and Discounts from Mozu into LightSpeed. After modifying your LightSpeed Retail inventory configurations, you can import data into Mozu to sync orders and inventory amounts.

Note: This app only supports the LightSpeed Retail product.

Install Mozu LightSpeed Retail Application on Your Tenant

After purchasing the Mozu LightSpeed Retail Application, call your sales or professional services representative and request installation on your tenant. Mozu staff will install the app to your tenant, making it available for further configuration and usage in your storefront.

This custom app integrates LightSpeed Retail with Mozu to support the following:

  • Export and import information from Mozu to your LightSpeed Retail account to manage orders and inventory as shoppers make purchases through the storefront.
  • Update contacts, orders, inventory, and products with categories between both systems to leverage features in Mozu and LightSpeed Retail.
  • Allow you to continue using LightSpeed Retail reporting and analytics against Mozu products and purchases.

When a shopper submits an order and completes payment, the following occurs between Mozu and the LightSpeed app:

  • LightSpeed Retail verifies and processes the order inventory amounts and payment information. If the inventory exists per the entered requests (in-store, online to deliver at home, etc) and the payment information processes, LightSpeed Retail updates the order status and inventory amounts.
  • At the configured time and frequency per day, the completed order data is imported into Mozu from LightSpeed Retail.
  • Mozu updates all inventory based on the configured locations, amounts, and purchase/delivery requirements. For example, inventory from a warehouse for online delivery versus in-store pickup from an in-store inventory of a brick and mortar storefront.
  • As data updates in Mozu, it is exported to LightSpeed Retail, including changes to products and categories, customer entries, and discounts.

Configuration Requirements

To configure LightSpeed Retail to work seamlessly with Mozu, you need specific items installed and settings provided. This guide details each step from requirements to final configuration for reviewing order for legitimacy and fraud potential.

You must meet the following criteria to configure the LightSpeed Retail Connector app:

  • You must have the Mozu LightSpeed Retail Application installed on your tenant.
  • You must have an active LightSpeed Retail account.

Configure the Application

To integrate your LightSpeed services with Mozu, you must authorize Mozu to access your LightSpeed account.

  1. From the Mozu Admin page, select Settings > Applications.
  2. Click the Mozu LightSpeed Retail Application.
  3. Click the Configuration link to open configuration settings.
  4. On the Settings tab, click Add LightSpeed Account.
  5. Select a Mozu Site to link to a LightSpeed Retail account, and then click the Authorize Mozu to Access LightSpeed button.
  6. On the LightSpeed Retail login page, sign in with your LightSpeed credentials to authorize Mozu to use your LightSpeed Retail account.
  7. Once you've authorized Mozu to use your LightSpeed Retail account, map your Mozu site to your LightSpeed Account. Under the Map Site to LightSpeed Account header, use the drop-down menus to select your Mozu Site and the appropriate LightSpeed Account.
  8. Under Map Mozu Locations to LightSpeed Shops, select the Mozu Location and map it to a LightSpeed Shop from the drop-down menus.
  9. Select the Import Frequency for Sales Import. This sets the amount of times per day LightSpeed exports data to Mozu. Options include Disabled, minutes 1 to 30, 1 hour, 8 hours, 12 hours, and Daily.
  10. Click Save. This step connects and configures Mozu to your LightSpeed Retail account.

Enable Mozu LightSpeed Retail Application

  1. On the Mozu Admin top bar, select Settings > Applications.
  2. Click Mozu LightSpeed Retail Application.
  3. Click Enable App on the LightSpeed page.

Export Data From Mozu to LightSpeed Retail

With your Mozu and LightSpeed Retail accounts connected and configured, you should perform an export of data to LightSpeed. This data sets the available products, inventory amounts, and data for orders including customer records and available discounts.

As you make changes to your catalog, customer records, and discounts, the event system keeps LightSpeed updated with add, delete, and update information.

The tab displays the selectable data types to export, a manual export button, and a refresh option for the table. The table displays an entry of exported data per data type with the date/time, number of records exported, status, and a linked error log. If no errors occur, the link is 0 without data to display. The status options include in-progress, failed, and completed.

  1. On the Mozu Admin top bar, select Settings > Applications.
  2. Click Mozu LightSpeed Retail Application.
  3. Click the Configuration link to open configuration settings.
  4. Click the Data Export tab.
  5. Select what data you want to export to LightSpeed Retail from Mozu: Products/Categories, Customers, and/or Discounts. Selecting all options is recommended.
  6. Click Export Now.
  7. An export job begins for each data type, posting an entry in the table with the date/time, number of records exported, status, and a linked error log.

Import Data From LightSpeed Retail to Mozu

The data imported from LightSpeed Retail is maintained on an automated schedule. This schedule is determined by the frequency configuration in the Settings tab. The next scheduled import is displayed with a date and time. The imported data is for completed sales which updates order, customer, and inventory data in Mozu.

The table includes an entry for every import listed by date and time. The number of sales is listed. If no sales are imported, the amount is listed as 0. The status is displayed as in progress, failed, or completed. A linked report of errors provides details on each import.

You can import data at the scheduled time or on-demand by clicking Import Now.

  1. On the Mozu Admin top bar, select Settings > Applications.
  2. Click Mozu LightSpeed Retail Application.
  3. Click the Configuration link to open configuration settings.
  4. Click the Data Import tab.
  5. Click Import Now.
  6. An import job begins from LightSpeed Retail, posting an entry in the table with the date/time, number of sales, status, and a linked error log.
