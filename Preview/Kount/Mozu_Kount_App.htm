Welcome to Kount and Mozu

Kount provides online services to determine the legitimacy and potential risk of shopper orders submitted through your storefront, specifically at the order level. The service reviews every submitted order for pertinent data including the shopper’s identity, the initial order submitted, the IP and origin location, and the device and browser to calculate a fraud score. Kount compares and assesses the risk of the order’s data and score per the rules you configure to identify legitimate and potentially fraudulent orders.

Mozu offers an app to leverage Kount’s rules and fraud detection to review your shoppers’ orders submitted through your storefront. All orders are determined as accepted, rejected, and failed according to the rules you configure and Kount’s services.

Application Features:

  • Sync your Kount account with Mozu to review orders submitted through your storefront.
  • Validate shopper purchases against your Kount rules to determine fraud scores and legitimacy of the purchaser.
  • Update the order status as pass/fail/rejected per the calculated fraud score and your Kount rules in Mozu.
  • Provide orders to review and manage through Mozu with Kount’s final determination.

When a shopper purchases their order, the following occurs between Mozu and the Kount app:

  • Mozu sends collected data to Kount including the browser used, desktop or device used to enter the order, order origin address, the IP of the purchasing system, and additional data as needed.
  • Kount verifies the legitimacy of the order against set rules on your Kount account. Kount determines the IP location, validity of the payment method, customer data, browser data, and further items.
  • Kount calculates a fraud score for the order and returns the order's status as accepted, failed, or rejected. If rejected, the order data did not clearly calculate as accepted or failed. You can review and actively accept or reject these orders through Mozu.
  • Mozu notifies you when purchases have returned as failed or rejected. These orders do require your review and final actions to accept the payment or cancel the order due to potential fraudulent activity through the Orders section.

Install Kount Application by Mozu on Your Tenant

After purchasing the Kount Application by Mozu, call your sales or professional services representative and request installation on your tenant. Mozu staff will install the app to your tenant, making it available for further configuration and usage in your storefront.

Configure the Application

To integrate your Kount services with Mozu, review the configuration requirements to ensure you have everything you need to be successful before you begin, and then complete the steps in this section.

Configuration Requirements

  • You must have the Kount Application by Mozu installed into your tenant.
  • You must have an active Kount account.
  • You must obtain your merchant ID from your Kount account. This ID may be designated as Volusion (the ID number) when accessing your account.
  • You must obtain the Kount API key associated with your Kount account. This key provides the required authentication for Mozu to send and receive data from Kount.
  • You must create and configure your fraud score and purchasing rules in Kount.
  • You must have access to modify and update your storefront theme.

Configure Kount Account Settings

  1. On the Mozu Admin top bar, select Settings > Applications.
  2. Click Kount.
  3. Click the Configuration link to open configuration settings.
  4. Open the Account Settings tab.
  5. Enter your Kount Merchant ID.
  6. Enter your Kount API key.
  7. (Optional) Enable Enable Kount validation threshold to set a price threshold below which orders are not screened by Kount. For example, you may want Kount to only screen orders over $10 for fraud.
  8. Click Save. This step connects Mozu to your Kount account.
  9. Copy the URL provided on the Account Settings tab, for example: https://sample.site.mozu.com/KountExt. This URL directs the Kount widget to send order data to the Kount Web Console (where data collection occurs). Save this link for the next step of configuration.

Enable the Kount Application by Mozu

After configuration, enable the Kount Application in Mozu to apply its functionality to your tenant:

  1. On the Mozu Admin top bar, select Settings > Applications.
  2. Click Kount.
  3. Click Enable App on the Kount page.

Enable Event Notification System

With the systems connected, you need to enable the Event Notification System (ENS) in your Kount account to synchronize data and enable actions with Mozu. On shopper purchases, Mozu processes the order and sends data to Kount, which calculates the fraud score and provides data back to Mozu.

While we highly recommend performing all order review and reporting directly through Mozu, you can use Kount services and sync any actions back into Mozu. Kount provides fraud score reviewing, management, and in-depth reports for purchases made through Mozu storefronts. You can review and approve the order within Kount, add history content, and update addresses. ENS syncs this data back into Mozu within a minute of entered changes.

Specific data changed within Kount syncs to Mozu including edits to the content, history, shipping and billing addresses, and state changes such as from review (R) to approve (A). If you change the state of an order in Kount, additional processes enact in Mozu when synced as if you had made that change directly in Mozu.

  1. In your Kount account site, select Fraud Control tab and select Websites.
  2. You will have a “DEFAULT” website available in the list. You can edit this website using the gear icon. Select Edit.
  3. Select Website Enabled and ENS Enabled as Yes.
  4. For the Merchant ENS URL, enter https://integrations2-{su}.mozu.com/Kount2/ens/{tenantId}, where {su} is the scale unit (sb, tp1, tp2, tp3, tp4, etc.). A Mozu support representative can help you identify the scale unit and the correct URL to use for your sandbox and production needs. An example of a Merchant ENS URL is https://integrations2-tp1.mozu.com/Kount2/ens/12345.
  5. Click Update Website.
